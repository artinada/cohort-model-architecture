## Insights
The overall cohort analysis shows a relatively stable user retention rate, with a gradual decline in activity and the presence of a long-term core user base. \
However, aggregated retention masks a significant difference in user behavior depending on the acquisition channel: \
users from non-promotional channels demonstrate significantly better behavior, while promotional traffic is characterized by low and unstable retention. \
Thus, analysis without segmentation can lead to an overestimation of user retention quality.

### 1. Retention patterns
Some cohorts within Non-promo channel show an anomaly in retention growth in subsequent periods, which may be due to the specifics of how the metric is calculated or to deferred user activity. \
Retention metrics for new cohorts appear higher; however, this may be due to incomplete data and does not allow for definitive conclusions regarding product improvement. \
Additionally, high retention values may indicate a broad definition of activity (which is indeed the case according to the task’s conditions). \

### 2. Channel comparison
Non-promo users show consistently higher retention and form a stable long-term user base \
Promo users demonstrate significantly lower retention and rapid drop-off after the first month \
Aggregated retention masks substantial differences between user segments.

### 3. Cohort trends
Some cohorts show non-monotonic retention patterns, suggesting delayed engagement or methodological limitations. \
No clear, stable trend of improvement or deterioration is observed.

### 4. Data limitations
Activity is defined as any user event (may overestimate retention) \
Possible use of rolling retention instead of strict retention \
Recent cohorts are incomplete (right censoring)
